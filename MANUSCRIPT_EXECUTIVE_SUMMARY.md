## 🎯 What This Manuscript Provides

This comprehensive review synthesizes all available evidence on antibody-drug conjugate (ADC)-associated interstitial lung disease (ILD) through January 2025, providing the definitive clinical practice guide for managing this dose-limiting and potentially fatal toxicity. The manuscript integrates clinical trial data, real-world studies, mechanistic research, and expert consensus to deliver actionable guidance for detection, treatment, and prevention.

**Key Contributions:**
- First comprehensive synthesis of ILD data across all approved and investigational ADCs with direct comparison of risk profiles
- Detailed mechanistic framework explaining payload-specific toxicity, Fcγ receptor-mediated macrophage uptake, and immune-mediated injury pathways
- Evidence-based treatment protocols stratified by toxicity grade with documented 76.9% overall recovery rate
- Safety data supporting rechallenge after grade 1 ILD recovery (6.4% recurrence rate, no fatal events in pooled analysis)
- Identification of lower-risk sequential ADC pathways based on structural differences (deruxtecan to sacituzumab govitecan)
- Emerging biomarker evidence for early detection (lung-derived cfDNA, serum KL-6, surfactant protein-D)
- Systematic documentation of the 70% reduction in mortality achieved through proactive monitoring (T-DXd: 2.7% to 0.8% grade 5 rate)

## 📊 Key Statistics & Findings

**ILD Incidence by ADC:**
- Highest risk: Trastuzumab deruxtecan - 10-28% (15.4% pooled clinical trials, 28.1% intensive real-world monitoring)
- High risk: Datopotamab deruxtecan - 8.8%, Mirvetuximab soravtansine - 10%
- Moderate risk: Enfortumab vedotin - 3% monotherapy, 10% with pembrolizumab; Tisotumab vedotin - 3%
- Lowest risk: Sacituzumab govitecan - <1%, Brentuximab vedotin - <1%, Ado-trastuzumab emtansine - <1%

**Clinical Outcomes:**
- Overall ILD mortality: 26% across all ADCs and grades
- Mortality with proactive monitoring: Reduced from 2.7% to 0.8% for T-DXd (70% reduction)
- Overall recovery rate: 76.9% for drug-related ILD with appropriate management
- Grade 1-2 mortality: <1% with appropriate management
- Grade 4 30-day mortality: 78% if mechanical ventilation required; 6-month mortality: 96%
- Organizing pneumonia pattern: <5% mortality, generally steroid-responsive
- Diffuse alveolar damage pattern: 42.1% mortality, often steroid-refractory

**Mechanistic Insights:**
- Non-specific macrophage uptake via Fcγ receptors independent of target antigen drives lung accumulation—T-DXd concentrates in lung tissue at levels comparable to tumor despite absence of HER2 expression on pneumocytes
- Topoisomerase I inhibitor payloads (deruxtecan platform) confer highest risk through high membrane permeability enabling bystander pneumocyte killing, while structurally similar SN-38 payload shows minimal toxicity due to different linker chemistry
- SPP1 (osteopontin) pathway identified as key mediator of macrophage activation and fibrotic progression, offering potential therapeutic target for prevention and treatment beyond corticosteroids

## 🔬 What Makes This Review Unique

**Comprehensive Cross-ADC Analysis:** Unlike prior single-agent reviews, this manuscript systematically compares ILD incidence, severity, and outcomes across all 15 approved ADCs, identifying payload class (topoisomerase I inhibitors > maytansinoids > auristatins) and linker chemistry (cleavable > non-cleavable) as primary determinants of lung toxicity. The direct comparison of trastuzumab deruxtecan (10-28% ILD) versus ado-trastuzumab emtansine (<1% ILD)—identical antibodies with different linkers/payloads—provides definitive evidence that ADC structure, not target antigen, drives pulmonary toxicity.

**Mechanistic Depth With Clinical Translation:** The manuscript bridges basic science and clinical practice by connecting preclinical mechanistic insights (Fcγ receptor-mediated macrophage uptake demonstrated in Kumagai's cynomolgus monkey studies, SPP1 pathway identification in 2024 studies) to actionable clinical implications. For example, the Fcγ receptor mechanism explains why different ADCs targeting different antigens produce similar ILD patterns and suggests future ADC designs with Fcγ-null antibodies to reduce toxicity.

**Evidence of Monitoring Impact:** This review quantifies the dramatic 70% reduction in trastuzumab deruxtecan mortality (2.7% to 0.8% grade 5 rate) achieved through enhanced baseline CT screening, frequent surveillance imaging, and rapid intervention protocols. This represents one of oncology's clearest demonstrations that systematic attention to toxicity management improves therapeutic index without compromising efficacy—a roadmap for managing future novel therapy complications.

**Sequential ADC Safety Guidance:** The manuscript addresses the critical real-world question of sequential ADC use by synthesizing limited available data and structural rationale. The identification of lower-risk pathways (deruxtecan-based ADC followed by sacituzumab govitecan with <1% ILD incidence) versus higher-risk combinations (sequential deruxtecan-based agents with shared payload) provides practical guidance absent from existing literature and prescribing information.

## 🎓 Key Takeaways (1-Minute Summary)

**The Problem:** ADC-associated ILD is a dose-limiting toxicity with 0.4%-28% incidence depending on ADC structure and monitoring intensity, carrying 26% overall mortality and 78% mortality for grade 4 disease requiring mechanical ventilation.

**What We Know Now:**
1. Topoisomerase I inhibitor payloads (deruxtecan platform) confer highest risk (10-28% incidence) through Fcγ receptor-mediated macrophage uptake and bystander pneumocyte killing—linker chemistry and payload properties matter more than target antigen
2. Proactive monitoring with baseline CT, surveillance imaging every 6-12 weeks, and grade-specific corticosteroid protocols achieve 76.9% overall recovery rate and reduced mortality by 70% (T-DXd: 2.7% to 0.8%)
3. Rechallenge after grade 1 ILD resolution is safe (6.4% recurrence, no fatal events), while grade 2+ requires permanent discontinuation under current evidence

**What Clinicians Should Do:**
1. Obtain baseline HRCT and pulmonary function tests before initiating high-risk ADCs (trastuzumab deruxtecan, datopotamab deruxtecan, mirvetuximab soravtansine)
2. Implement surveillance imaging every 6-12 weeks with immediate ADC discontinuation and corticosteroid initiation upon ILD detection
3. Apply grade-specific management: Grade 1 allows observation or low-dose steroids with possible rechallenge; Grade 2+ requires 1-2 mg/kg/day prednisone, permanent discontinuation, and consideration of alternative ADCs like sacituzumab govitecan

**What Researchers Should Study:**
1. Prospective validation of lung-derived cfDNA and KL-6 biomarkers for earlier detection before symptomatic disease
2. Randomized trials of corticosteroid regimens and steroid-refractory treatment strategies (antifibrotics, immunosuppressants)
